The Palace

One day as the King wandered around his palace, he heard loud snoring sounds coming from a rose bush far away. Looking closer, he found a sleeping Satyr, which managed to get lost. The King knew that this Satyr was one of Dionysus' servants, and so King Midas let him stay in his palace till the Satyr felt well rested.

The Palace

His actions pleased Dionysus, who allowed the king to be granted one wish. A greedy king he was, King Midas requested for the powers to turn whatever he touched into gold. The god smiled and with a click of a finger, the king was granted his wish. The king went crazy, touching everything that came in his path, as he watched the glistening gold shine in his eyes.

The Palace

Running around the palace was exhausting, so the king reached out to eat grapes when he nearly shattered his teeth as the grapes turned to gold in his mouth. Shocked, he picked up a loaf of bread, which hardened instantly in his hand. While the king cried in frustration, his daughter walked into the room to comfort her father as she flung her hands around him.

The Palace

But to one touch, there stood a golden statue of the king’s daughter. Scared of what he had done, Midas turned and begged to the gods, ‘Please turn me back, get rid of this curse!’. Dionysus, amused by Midas’ misfortune, guided him to wash his hands in the River Pactolus. As Midas dipped his hands in the river, the gold powdered and drained away.

The Palace

Midas returned home to find his daughter alive and his palace back to normal, to the day he thanked the gods and promised never to be greedy again.

The End!
